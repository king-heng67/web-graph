Acceleration Theory

Motion is more than just moving from A to B. It's about how your velocity changes over time.

When velocity is constant, acceleration is exactly zero. The object covers the same distance every second.

Uniform Motion

Pure velocity means an object moves at a steady rate. If a car moves at 10m/s, it will always be 10m further ahead after each second.

In this state, the acceleration is zero because the "gas pedal" isn't being pressed or released. Net force is zero.

Nuclear Physics

Core Theory

Before understanding decay, we must understand the "Tug of War" inside every atom. Why do some nuclei stick together while others explode?

Protons have a positive charge. If you put them together, they want to fly apart!

Electrostatic Force

Coulomb's Law states that like charges repel. In a nucleus, protons are packed incredibly tight. The repulsive force trying to tear them apart is ENORMOUS.

Think of it like trying to hold two powerful magnets together with the same poles facing. They want to snap away from each other instantly.

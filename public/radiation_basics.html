Phase 1: Atomic Instability

Nuclear Decay

Radiation is a random process. Some nuclei have too much energy or an unbalanced ratio of neutrons to protons, making them unstable.

The Activity of a source is the rate at which it decays, measured in Becquerels (Bq). One Bq is one decay per second.

16 Unstable Nuclei. Each half-life, roughly half of the remaining unstable nuclei will decay.

Step 3: Environment

Background Radiation

Radiation isn't just in labs. It's all around us right now! This constant low-level radiation is called Natural Background Radiation.

It comes from the ground (Radon gas), space (Cosmic rays), and even the food you eat (Potassium-40 in bananas!). This is why sensors never read exactly zero.
